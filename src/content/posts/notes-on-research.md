The first year of a PhD is mostly reading. You knew this. Everyone tells you
this. What nobody tells you is that the reading isn't the hard part — the
hard part is the slow, quiet realization that the reading list grows faster
than you can shrink it, and that this is permanent.

You will not catch up. Make peace with that on day one and you'll save
yourself six months.

## a small discipline that helped

For about a month I kept a single text file open in a corner of my screen,
called `unknown.txt`. Whenever I read a paper and hit something I didn't
understand — a term, a technique, a citation I didn't recognize — I dumped
it into that file. No definition. No explanation. Just the word, the source,
and a one-line context.

By the end of the month the file had 240 lines.

The point wasn't to look things up later. The point was to stop pretending
I understood things I didn't. When you're new, the temptation to nod along
is enormous, even when nobody's watching. The file made it impossible to
nod along to myself.

## the only piece of advice I trust

> Pick a small problem you can hold in your head and turn it over while you
> walk to lunch. The good ideas come on the walk.

That's not original to me. I think it's Hamming, filtered through three
advisors. But it has been the only thing that's actually worked.
