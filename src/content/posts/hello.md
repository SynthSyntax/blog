I've started and abandoned three blogs before this one. Each time I quit for the
same reason: the platform got in the way. Either it nudged me toward writing
for an algorithm, or the editor had its own opinions about how I should feel,
or every post needed a hero image and a tagline and a SEO score.

So this one is different in the most boring way possible. It's a folder of
Markdown files. The styling is mine. The words are mine. There's no comment
section, no like button, no newsletter pop-up, no reading-time estimate
pretending to know how fast you read. If a post is good you'll know. If it
isn't you'll close the tab. Both are fine.

## what to expect

I do research in computer science and robotics, so a lot of this will be about
that — proofs that nearly worked, experiments that didn't, the gap between
what a paper says and what actually happens at 2am the night before the
deadline. But I also build things on the side, read too much, and have
opinions about software, so those will leak in too.

The tags are how to find what you came for:

- `#research` — PhD work, papers, ideas in progress.
- `#projects` — things I built that aren't research.
- `#thoughts` — essays, rants, slow walks.
- `#til` — small things learned, captured before I forget.

## the inspiration

I owe a debt to [Neil Fraser's blog](https://neil.fraser.name/). I don't share
his aesthetic — this place is dressier — but I share his stance: a blog is a
diary you let people read. Write what's true. Date the entries. Don't try to
sound smart.

That's the bar. Let's see how I do.
